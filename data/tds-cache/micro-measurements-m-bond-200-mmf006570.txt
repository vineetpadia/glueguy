Product: M-Bond 200 Strain Gage Adhesive, Micro-Measurements / Vishay Precision Group. Document No. 11010, Revision 17-May-2017. Description: cyanoacrylate adhesive pretested and certified for bonding strain gages, intended for routine experimental stress analysis under temperate environmental conditions. The datasheet says it is easy to handle, cures almost instantly, and produces an essentially creep-free, fatigue-resistant bond with elongation capability of five percent or more. It is an excellent general-purpose adhesive for laboratory and short-term field applications; detailed installation is in Instruction Bulletin B-127 included in each kit. Cure requirements: one-minute thumb pressure followed by a minimum two-minute delay before tape removal; bond strength increases rapidly during the first five minutes. Cure time must be extended below 70 F / 21 C or below 40 percent RH. Operating temperature range: short term -300 to +200 F / -185 to +95 C; long term -25 to +150 F / -32 to +65 C. Elongation capabilities: greater than 5 percent at +75 F / +24 C; 3 percent at +75 F / +24 C when used with CEA or EA/Option E strain gages. Shelf life: minimum 12 months unopened at +40 F / +5 C, or minimum 9 months unopened at +75 F / +24 C. Pot life after opening: maximum 3 months at +75 F / +24 C, not to exceed expiration date. Handling notes: wipe bottle spout clean and dry before replacing cap; condensation rapidly degrades adhesive performance and shelf life; allow refrigerated adhesive to reach room temperature before opening; refrigeration after opening is not recommended; replace cap immediately after each use. Packaging options: kit includes 1 bottle 1 oz / 28 g adhesive and 1 brush-cap bottle 30 mL catalyst; bulk options include 1 bottle 1 oz / 28 g adhesive, 5 tubes 2 g each adhesive, 16 bottles 1 oz / 28 g each adhesive, and 12 brush-cap bottles 30 mL each catalyst. The datasheet warns performance can be degraded by time, humidity conditions, elevated temperature and moisture absorption, so strain-gage installations should be covered with a suitable protective coating; for more rigorous test requirements or environments, consider M-Bond epoxy adhesives. The datasheet does not publish viscosity, lap shear, peel, tensile strength, hardness, dielectric properties, thermal conductivity, density, Tg or CTE.
